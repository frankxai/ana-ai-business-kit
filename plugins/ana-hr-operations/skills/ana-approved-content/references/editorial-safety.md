# Editorial safety and approval standard

## Required inputs

Every content brief needs:

- audience, format, intended value, and proposed channel;
- source entry IDs or Ana-approved source facts;
- draft title/message, factual boundaries, and one clear next step;
- explicit statement that the draft is not approved for publishing/sending;
- named Ana approval requirement and a separate human publication receipt if it later goes live.

## Source and claim rules

- Link every factual/technical/research claim to a reviewed source entry or mark it as an opinion/question.
- Preserve uncertainty and scope. Avoid absolute language unless an approved source supports it precisely.
- Do not use client stories, names, logos, quotes, metrics, testimonials, screenshots, or before/after claims without specific written permission and Ana's approval.
- Do not claim legal compliance, medical/psychological benefit, guaranteed hiring outcome, or an AI capability beyond what is demonstrably approved.

## Ethical persuasion check

Before Ana reviews, ask:

1. Is the problem described accurately and respectfully?
2. Is the value specific without a promise of outcome?
3. Does the audience understand scope, limits, and next step?
4. Is every proof point sourced and approved?
5. Would the draft remain fair if the reader says no?

If any answer is no, keep the draft blocked.

## Never do automatically

- publish, schedule, boost, email, DM, post, or share;
- use an unapproved client story or testimonial;
- create false scarcity, hidden persuasion, impersonation, or fabricated social proof;
- make neuro/psychology, health, employment, tax, legal, or compliance claims beyond reviewed and appropriate sources.
