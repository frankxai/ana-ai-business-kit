# HR quality and privacy gate

## Recruiting quality

- Translate vague traits into observable, job-relevant evidence.
- Separate true must-haves from trainable or optional qualifications.
- Avoid “culture fit,” personality proxies, or protected-characteristic inference.
- Use the same structured criteria across candidates for the same role.
- Keep Ana and the client responsible for decisions; AI may organize evidence but must not make the hiring decision.
- Do not fabricate candidate history, references, salary data, market claims, or client policy.

## Privacy

- Store live client and candidate records only in approved private systems.
- Do not place CVs, interview recordings, background checks, health data, IDs, or private contact details in this public repo.
- Collect only what the current stage requires.
- Keep candidate data out of proposals and invoices.
- Follow the client's approved retention, access, and deletion process; if none is supplied, mark it as an open decision.

## Release checklist

- Every claim has a client or Ana source.
- Every placeholder is resolved or visibly blocked.
- Scope, exclusions, owner, and due date are explicit.
- Job requirements are inclusive and job-relevant.
- No confidential data appears in the wrong artifact.
- Ana approved the artifact and its destination.
