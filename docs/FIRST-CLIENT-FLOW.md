# First client: call to invoice draft

Use one private engagement record per client and role. The record lives in company-approved private storage outside this GitHub repository.

## Before the call

Prepare:

- client company and primary contact;
- the business outcome and likely role or service need;
- known stakeholders and decision-makers;
- call date and target timing;
- Ana's approved document template;
- current approved pricing source, currency, tax treatment, payment terms, and billing identity.

It is fine for items to be unknown. Codex should track them as open decisions instead of guessing.

Start with:

> Use Ana HR Operations. Create a private engagement record outside this repository for one client and one role. Guide me through the first-call questions one section at a time. Capture facts, assumptions, missing decisions, owner, and due date separately. End with a readback for the client. Do not create an offer, invoice, email, or calendar event yet.

## Stage 1 — 20–30 minute first call

Capture:

1. Why the client is engaging Ana now.
2. The measurable business and role outcome.
3. Scope, exclusions, geography, language, and timing.
4. Decision-maker, daily contact, approvers, and availability.
5. Recruiting stages and job-relevant evidence.
6. Candidate-data handling and deletion expectations.
7. Required documents and Ana's exact Google Docs template.
8. Commercial inputs and which items still require Ana's decision.
9. Next owner and due date.

Finish with a short readback. Correct the record before advancing.

## Stage 2 — kickoff

Ask:

> Validate the first-call record for kickoff. Show complete facts, missing decisions, approvals, next owner, due date, and every external action that still requires me.

Approve the scope only when stakeholders, selection process, privacy expectations, cadence, template source, billing inputs, and next document are clear.

## Stage 3 — job description and service offer

For recruiting:

> Draft the job description from the approved kickoff. Use job-relevant criteria and structured evidence. Do not use protected traits or personality proxies. Keep it local until I approve the content and template route.

For the commercial offer:

> Draft the service offer from the approved kickoff. Separate scope, exclusions, deliverables, timing, dependencies, and commercial terms. Do not invent price, tax, currency, or payment terms.

The job description and offer may proceed together after kickoff approval. They remain drafts until Ana reviews them.

## Stage 4 — pricing approval

Codex must show the source for every amount and calculate:

- line-item quantity × unit price;
- subtotal;
- discount, if explicitly approved;
- tax basis, rate, and amount, if supplied;
- total and currency;
- deposit or milestone schedule;
- payment terms.

Use:

> Validate the offer totals against the approved pricing source. Show the arithmetic and list every missing commercial fact. Wait for my explicit approval before marking pricing approved.

## Stage 5 — invoice draft

An invoice can be drafted only from an approved offer or completed milestone.

Use:

> Create an invoice draft from the approved offer. Verify billing identities, invoice number, issue and due dates, currency, line items, subtotal, tax, total, payment terms, and payment details. Do not invent missing facts and do not send it.

Ana reviews the invoice and records explicit approval with the approver and timestamp.

## Stage 6 — send preparation

Use:

> Prepare the final handoff and email draft. The intended recipient is [exact recipient] through [exact channel]. Show me the attachment or document link and wait for my explicit send approval. Do not send in this turn.

Sending is a separate action. Ana must confirm the exact recipient, channel, attachment or link, and permission to send in the active task. A prior approval or silence is not enough.

## Definition of done

Codex returns:

- current stage;
- artifacts created or changed;
- facts used and assumptions blocked;
- validation result;
- approvals present and missing;
- Google Docs readback and layout-inspection status;
- the exact next human action.
