# Team starter prompts

These are copy/paste prompts for individual Codex tasks. Replace only private placeholders inside an approved private task. They are deliberately explicit about what must not happen.

## Shared opening line

Add this to every live-work prompt:

> Work only from the private records and exact sources I provide. Do not invent facts, search broadly for templates, retain candidate data outside the ATS, make a hiring decision, create a commercial commitment, publish, schedule, share, or send anything without a fresh named Ana approval.

## Ana — manager and approver

> Use Ana HR Operations. Run manager morning control across the private engagement records I provide. Show a privacy-safe table with client alias, stage, next action, owner, due date, blocker, approval needed, template status, and source-review timestamp. Flag stale, missing, privacy-sensitive, money, or send risks. Do not change a record or treat this review as approval.

> Use Ana HR Operations. Prepare an approval decision card for `[engagement alias]` and `[artifact]`. Show exact source facts, assumptions, calculation/reconciliation, latest revision, template/visual-check state, exact recipient/channel if relevant, and the precise approval requested. Do not record any approval until I state it explicitly.

## Engagement coordinator

> Use Ana HR Operations. Select SOP-01 for `[engagement alias]`. Guide me one section at a time through the private first-call capture. Keep facts, assumptions, owners, due dates, template route, and approval needs separate. End with a client readback and run the first-call validation. Do not create a final document, offer, invoice, meeting, or email.

> Use Ana HR Operations. Select SOP-02 for `[engagement alias]`. Reconcile the approved first-call facts into kickoff scope, exclusions, accountable people, cadence, structured process, data boundary, template route, commercial owners, and next actions. Identify every unresolved decision and return `TEMPLATE_BLOCKED` when the exact master route is missing.

## Research and recruiting operator

> Use Ana HR Operations. Select SOP-03 for `[engagement alias]`. Create a role scorecard and job-description draft from approved kickoff facts. Turn vague traits into observable job-relevant evidence or mark them blocked. Keep candidate information in the ATS. Do not rank, reject, or select candidates.

> Use Ana HR Operations. Select SOP-05 for `[engagement alias]`. Prepare an aggregate-only recruiting status from the approved ATS reference. Show evidence/process learning, risks, client decisions needed, change requests, and next seven-day plan. Do not include candidate names, CV content, interview notes, or recommendations.

> Use Ana Research Library. Capture `[source]` for `[question]`. Separate the source's actual claim from our interpretation, rate evidence strength, list scope/limits, state applicability, and name the human who must decide whether to use it. Do not turn team or neuroscience research into hiring, performance, diagnosis, or profiling advice.

## Document and client-experience operator

> Use Ana HR Operations. Create a content pack for `[kickoff / job description / offer / invoice / presentation]` for `[engagement alias]`. Use only approved sources. List every fact, number source, unresolved field, template ID, destination, required approval, and visual-check requirement. Do not create or share a final document yet.

> Use Ana HR Operations and Google Docs. Using the exact private master and destination I provide, create a copy only, map approved content in small verified batches, re-read it, and report the copy URL, structure/readback, remaining placeholders, and rendered-layout verification status. Keep it a draft and do not share or send it.

> Use Ana HR Operations and Canva. Use the exact private master I provide, create a copy only, apply the approved content pack, and preserve the master visual system. Mark `CANVA_RENDER_PENDING` until I record a human visual check. Do not export, publish, share, or send it.

> Use Ana Approved Content. Draft `[format]` from approved source entries only. State the intended audience, source IDs, factual boundaries, clear next step, and approval needed. Do not use a client story, guarantee, urgency tactic, diagnosis, neuroscience claim, publish, or send.

## An honest persuasive offer

For an offer or client-facing page, use this additional instruction:

> Make the value proposition specific and credible: name the client outcome, scope, exclusions, timing, dependencies, and next step in plain language. Do not claim a guaranteed hire, fabricate scarcity, make unsupported market claims, or turn uncertainty into certainty.

## When a prompt must stop

Stop and ask Ana/owner when any of these is missing: exact master template, destination/copy approval, accountable client decision-maker, approved source for a factual claim, pricing/tax source, invoice-number source, explicit approval, exact recipient/channel, or approved data boundary.
