# Fictional practice engagement

Run this before a live client, when a teammate joins, or after a material process update. Every name, number, and link below is fictional. Do not replace the fictional data with real client or candidate material during the practice.

## Scenario

**Client alias:** `EXAMPLE-OPS`

**Fictional client:** Example Company B.V.

**Need:** hire a Senior Operations Lead and establish a more structured hiring process.

**Business context:** a growing professional-services firm needs dependable operating rhythm, clear stakeholder coordination, and a six-week initial search.
**Team practice extension:** the client is considering a voluntary team-collaboration workshop after the hire. Ana may research facilitation practices and team working agreements, but no workshop claim becomes medical, neuroscience, diagnostic, or candidate-selection advice.

Use the public-safe record at [engagement.example.json](../plugins/ana-hr-operations/skills/ana-hr-operations/assets/engagement.example.json). Copy it only to a private practice folder if the team needs to edit it.

## Roles for this exercise

| Role | Practice responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| Ana | Run manager controls; approve only stated fictional decisions |
| Engagement coordinator | Run first-call and kickoff capture, set next owner/date |
| Research and recruiting operator | Prepare job-relevant scorecard/process and research entry |
| Document and client-experience operator | Prepare content packs and verify draft/template status |

## Runbook

### 1. Manager morning control — SOP-00

Ana uses the manager prompt in [Start Here](../START-HERE-ANA.md). Expected outcome: one alias-only row, one next action, one owner, a due date, and no candidate identity.

Pass when the board distinguishes `TEMPLATE_BLOCKED` or approval needs from completed work.

### 2. First call — SOP-01

The coordinator asks:

> Use Ana HR Operations. Open the fictional `EXAMPLE-OPS` record and run SOP-01 as a practice. Capture only the supplied facts, identify missing assumptions, assign owners and dates, and validate the first-call stage. Do not create an offer, invoice, email, calendar event, or client document.

Pass when the first-call validation succeeds and open decisions remain visibly open.

### 3. Kickoff — SOP-02

The coordinator asks:

> Use Ana HR Operations. Run SOP-02 for the fictional record. Confirm scope, exclusions, decision-makers, structured process, data boundary, cadence, template route, and commercial owners. The real template URLs are intentionally unavailable, so report `TEMPLATE_BLOCKED`; do not substitute another template.

Ana then explicitly records the fictional kickoff approval.

### 4. Role and recruiting design — SOP-03 and SOP-05

The research/recruiting operator asks:

> Use Ana HR Operations. Run SOP-03 for the fictional Senior Operations Lead. Create a job-relevant scorecard and job-description draft from the approved facts. Replace any vague trait with observable evidence or mark it blocked. Do not rank or decide on candidates.

Then:

> Use Ana HR Operations. Run SOP-05 as a practice. Produce only aggregate pipeline counts and a weekly status draft. Keep all candidate identities and evidence in the fictional ATS reference; do not put them in this record.

Pass when the process is structured, job-relevant, and aggregate-only.

### 5. Offer and invoice — SOP-04 and SOP-06

The document operator asks:

> Use Ana HR Operations. Run SOP-04 for the fictional engagement. Reconcile scope, exclusions, source-linked fictional price inputs, tax, payment terms, and offer wording. Show the arithmetic and wait for the separate fictional price and offer approvals. Keep template status blocked because no actual master was supplied.

After Ana gives the fictional price and offer approvals:

> Use Ana HR Operations. Run SOP-06. Create only a visibly labeled invoice draft from the approved fictional offer. Reconcile billing identity, number source, dates, currency, line items, tax, total, terms, and finance-reference placeholder. Do not create a payment, alter an accounting record, share, or send.

Pass when the calculation validates and the invoice remains a draft.

### 6. Canva and Google Docs visual route

The document operator prepares a fictional content pack using [Template and Canva routing](TEMPLATE-AND-CANVA-ROUTING.md). No real master is provided in this exercise.

Pass when the output says `TEMPLATE_BLOCKED` rather than pretending that a Google Doc or Canva design exists. If Ana uses a disposable internal practice master, it must still be copied and visually checked by a named human.

### 7. Research library

The research operator asks:

> Use Ana Research Library. Capture a research entry about structured, job-relevant interviewing or voluntary team working agreements. Classify the source, separate fact from interpretation, identify applicability and limits, and explain why it must not be used for candidate profiling, diagnosis, or automated selection.

Pass when the entry has a source, claim, context, applicability, owner, review date, and a human decision boundary.

### 8. Approval-gated content

The document operator asks:

> Use Ana Approved Content. Draft a short, accurate educational post about structured hiring or team collaboration from an approved research entry. Use no client story, outcome guarantee, urgency tactic, or medical/neuroscience claim. Mark it `DRAFT_REVIEW_REQUIRED` and do not publish or send it.

Pass when a content draft is separate from publication approval.

## Practice acceptance checklist

- [ ] Four role owners have used their own prompt.
- [ ] Every stage has an SOP receipt.
- [ ] Real client/candidate/financial data never appears.
- [ ] A missing template becomes `TEMPLATE_BLOCKED`.
- [ ] No Canva visual review is claimed without a human check.
- [ ] Ana approval is named and specific; no approval is inferred.
- [ ] A candidate is never ranked, diagnosed, profiled, or selected by AI.
- [ ] The research and content skills produce a draft plus boundary, not a live action.

If any box is not true, repeat only the failed stage before a live pilot.
